# Impeccable design context

Single source of truth for visual and UX direction. Update this when audience or positioning changes.

## Design Context

### Users

Small internal team (**about 1–20 colleagues**) using the tool during **AACR 2026** prep and on-site. Primary devices are **laptop** and **phone** while moving (hallways, transit, between sessions). Core jobs: **quick discovery** of relevant abstracts and **session planning** (building a mental or exported schedule from the program).

### Brand Personality

**Organized, focused, visual** — dense information should feel scannable and under control, not overwhelming. **Fast and playful** — interactions and feedback should feel responsive and lightly spirited, not stiff or corporate, while still appropriate for research content.

### Aesthetic Direction

- **References:** [diabrowser.com](https://diabrowser.com) — spatial, confident browsing energy; strong visual hierarchy; interface that feels intentional and product-native rather than “default dashboard.”
- **Anti-references:** **Linear** — avoid cold, ultra-minimal issue-tracker neutrality, muted sameness, and UI that feels interchangeable with generic SaaS. Prefer warmth, clearer personality, and map-first visual identity over flat lists-as-product.
- **Theme:** Support **both light and dark** with a durable system (user preference, respects `prefers-color-scheme` as default). Neither mode should be an afterthought.
- **Color and perception:** Meet **accessibility** expectations for contrast and focus/keyboard use. Treat **color vision** as a real constraint where feasible: do not rely on color alone for meaning (pair hue with labels, patterns, weight, or position). Optional enhancements (e.g. patterns, settings) are welcome when they do not clutter the fast path.

### Design Principles

1. **Mobile-first clarity** — Touch targets, readable type, and collapsible chrome so laptop and phone sessions feel equally usable on the move.
2. **Map as home** — The 3D semantic view stays the hero; tables and panels support it rather than replacing it.
3. **Speed over decoration** — Playfulness shows up in motion, microcopy, and feedback latency, not in heavy ornament that slows scanning.
4. **Honest density** — Organized layouts and grouping beat sparse placeholders; empty states still teach the next action.
5. **Accessible by default** — Contrast, focus visibility, and redundant encoding for color-coded clusters/topics where it matters for decisions.
